THE ISRO JOURNEY

ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the national space agency of India. It operates as the primary research and development arm of the Department of Space (DoS), which is directly overseen by the Prime Minister of India, while the Chairman of ISRO also acts as the executive of DoS. ISRO is primarily responsible for performing tasks related to space-based operations, space exploration, international space cooperation and the development of related technologies. It is one of the 6 largest space agencies in the world today. ISRO maintains one of the biggest fleets of remote sensing (IRS) and communication (INSAT) satellites catering to the needs of the nation through a network of centres, offices, and research institutes in different parts of the country.

TIMELINE

Establishment of ISRO
ISRO was founded on August 15, 1969, by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, the visionary scientist and space pioneer.
Aryabhata Satellite
India's first satellite launched.
Rohini Satellite
The Rohini series of satellites was launched, beginning with Rohini Satellite RS-1 in 1980.
SLV (Satellite Launch Vehicle)
India's first indigenously developed launch vehicle, SLV-3, successfully launched the Rohini Satellite RS-1.
INSAT Series
The first satellite in the Indian National Satellite System (INSAT), INSAT-1A, was launched in 1982. The INSAT series provided communication, broadcasting, and meteorological services.
ASLV (Augmented Satellite Launch Vehicle)
India successfully launched its Augmented Satellite Launch Vehicle (ASLV) for the first time.
PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle)
The PSLV, known for its versatility, successfully launched the IRS-1B satellite in 1992. It has since become a workhorse for ISRO.
Chandrayaan-1
India's first lunar mission, Chandrayaan-1, was launched on October 22, 2008. It helped discover water molecules on the Moon's surface.
Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan)
On November 5, 2013, ISRO's Mars Orbiter Mission, also known as Mangalyaan, successfully entered Martian orbit. India became the fourth space agency in the world to reach Mars and the first to do so on its maiden attempt.
GSLV Mk III
The Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle Mark III, also known as LVM-3, had its successful maiden flight. It is capable of launching heavier payloads to geostationary orbits.
Reusable Launch Vehicle (RLV-TD)
ISRO conducted its first successful test flight of a Reusable Launch Vehicle Technology Demonstrator (RLV-TD) as a step toward reducing launch costs.
Record Satellite Launch
ISRO set a world record by launching 104 satellites in a single mission using the PSLV-C37 rocket.
Chandrayaan-2
India's Chandrayaan-2 mission, which included an orbiter, lander (Vikram), and rover (Pragyan), aimed to explore the Moon's south pole region. While the lander's attempt to soft-land on the Moon was not successful, the orbiter continues to study the lunar surface.
Gaganyaan
ISRO initiated its human spaceflight program, known as Gaganyaan, with plans to send Indian astronauts into space.
Mars Orbiter Mission 2 (Mangalyaan-2)
ISRO announced plans for a second mission to Mars, building on the success of Mangalyaan.
Chandrayaan-3
Chadrayaan-3,the third mission in the chandrayaan programme was launched on 14 July 2023.
